Homemade Mozzarella Cheese

Description

Homemade mozzarella cheese is easy to make from scratch. Now you can learn how to make it from scratch to enjoy it fresh on your pizzas, pasta, or however you like!

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup of cool water, divided
  • 1/4 teaspoon of liquid rennet
    • <1i>1 1/2 teaspoons of citric acid <1i>1 gallon of raw milk
  • 1 teaspoon of salt (optional)

Steps

  1. Combine 1/4 cup of water and rennet in a small bowl and mix well.
  2. Combine 1/2 cup of water and citric acid in another small bowl; mix until dissolved
  3. Pour milk into a large pot set over medium heat. Stir in the water-citric acid mixture. Heat the milk, stirring occasionally, until it registers 90 degrees Fahrenheit on an instant-read thermometer, about 5 minutes.
  4. Remove the milk from heat and stir in the rennet mixture in a figure-8 motion for 30 seconds. Stir counterclockwise for 30 seconds to still the milk. Let it stand, covered, until milk sets into curd, about 5 to 10 minutes. Press the edge of the curd gently with a palette knife or the back of a spoon to check for firmness.
  5. Slice verticaly into the pot to cut the curd into 3/4-inch cubes with a palette knife. Stir curds gently, but leave the cubes mostly intact.
  6. Return the pot to heat; cook over medium heat, stirring the curds gently until the temperature reaches 109 degrees Fahrenheit, about 5 minutes. Remove from the heat.
  7. Ladle the curds into a colander set over a large bowl using a slotted spoon. Press the curds gently to extract liquid whey. Pour the drained whey back into the pot.
  8. Heat whey to 185 degrees Fahrenheit, about 5 minutes. While wearing gloves, tear off a piece of curd and place on a slotted spoon. Dip the curd into hot whey for 5 to 10 seconds. Stretch, fold, and knead the curd. Repeat until the mozzarella is smooth and elastic. Knead in salt and form curd into a ball. Repeat dipping and kneading process with the remaining curd.
  9. Allow the mozzarella to cool, then wrap tightly in plastic wrap and transfer to the refrigerator.
